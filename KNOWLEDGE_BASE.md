### 2. Diagnostik (Diagnostics)

**EKG (Electrocardiogram):**
- Resting ECG
- Exercise ECG (Belastungs-EKG) on bicycle ergometer
- Detects heart rhythm disorders, coronary heart disease

**Sonographie (Ultrasound):**
- Abdominal organs (liver, gallbladder, kidneys, spleen, pancreas)
- Thyroid gland
- Lymph nodes
- Blood vessels (carotid arteries, leg veins)
- Soft tissues and joints

**24-Hour Blood Pressure Monitoring:**
- Automatic measurements over 24 hours
- Diagnosis and therapy control of hypertension
- Detection of nocturnal blood pressure changes

**Lung Function Tests:**
- Spirometry for asthma, COPD diagnosis

**Other Diagnostic Tests:**
- 24-hour ECG (Holter monitoring)
- Blood glucose measurement
- Rapid tests (CRP, Troponin, D-Dimer)
- Urine analysis
- Wound swabs

---

### 3. Laboruntersuchungen (Laboratory Tests)

**Routine Laboratory:**
- Complete blood count (gro√ües/kleines Blutbild)
- Blood glucose and HbA1c (long-term blood sugar)
- Cholesterol and lipid profile
- Liver and kidney values
- Thyroid values (TSH, fT3, fT4)
- Vitamins and minerals (Vitamin D, B12, Iron)
- Inflammatory markers (CRP, ESR)

**Special Diagnostics:**
- Hormone tests
- Tumor markers
- Allergy tests
- Covid-19 PCR and antigen tests

**Procedure:**
- Blood draw in practice
- Fasting required for some tests (12 hours)
- Results typically within 1-3 days
- Detailed discussion of results

### 4. Vorsorgeuntersuchungen (Preventive Care)

**Check-up 35:**
- Available for patients 35+ every 3 years
- Comprehensive health examination
- Includes: detailed medical history, physical exam, blood pressure, blood tests (cholesterol, glucose), urine test
- Early detection of cardiovascular diseases, kidney disease, diabetes

**Hautkrebs-Screening (Skin Cancer Screening):**
- From age 35, every 2 years
- Full-body skin examination for suspicious changes

**Cancer Screening:**
- Colorectal cancer screening (stool test, colonoscopy)
- Prostate cancer screening (men 45+)
- Breast cancer screening (mammography for women)
- Cervical cancer screening

**Individual Health Services (IGeL):**
- Extended laboratory tests
- Preventive ultrasound examinations
- Extended cardiovascular diagnostics
- Vitamin and mineral analysis
- Travel medicine consultation

### 6. Reisemedizin (Travel Medicine)

**Services:**
- Comprehensive travel health consultation
- Country-specific health risk information
- Vaccination recommendations
- Malaria prophylaxis
- Travel pharmacy assembly

**When to Book:**
- Ideally **3-4 months before departure**
- Short-notice appointments possible if needed
- Travel vaccination consultation should always be done before travel

**Malaria Prophylaxis:**
- Chemoprophylaxis (preventive medication)
- Standby therapy (emergency medication)
- Advice on mosquito bite protection (exposure prophylaxis)

**Travel Pharmacy Recommendations:**
- Pain and fever medication
- Anti-diarrheal and anti-nausea medication
- Disinfectant and bandaging material
- Sun protection and insect repellent
- Regular medications in sufficient quantity
- Motion sickness medication
- Electrolyte solutions

**Special Groups:**
- Pregnant and breastfeeding women
- Infants and children
- Seniors
- Chronically ill patients
- Immunocompromised individuals
- Long-term travelers and emigrants

**Cost:** 
- Generally private billing (not covered by statutory health insurance)
- Some health insurance companies reimburse costs partially or fully

### Emergency Situations

**Q: What do I do in a medical emergency after hours?**
A: Call 116 117 for medical emergency service or 112 for life-threatening emergencies.

**Q: Can I contact the practice after hours?**
A: For urgent matters after hours, use emergency services. The practice can be reached during normal hours.

**Q: What constitutes a medical emergency?**
A: Chest pain, severe breathing difficulty, sudden severe bleeding, loss of consciousness, stroke symptoms (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty).
